    \subsection*{Some notes on burritos}
\end{center}

When we want to take the derivative of a composite function $f(g(x))$, we use the Chain Rule:
\[\dfrac{d}{dx}\Big[f(g(x))\Big] = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)\]
Composite functions are like burritos: they are made of a tortilla wrapped around a filling. In the setup $f(g(x))$, I am saying that the filling is $g(x)$ and the tortilla is $f(\squiggle)$.
